

We’re stepping into March, and spring is almost here. Now, here in Washington, we don’t get the dramatic ‘dead‑to‑alive’ transformation other places see. Our trees stay green, our forests stay full, and the landscape never really looks asleep.

But spring still does something important: it _reveals_ what’s been growing quietly beneath the surface. Buds start to swell, new shoots push through the soil, and what was hidden becomes visible.

Spring reminds us that God often places potential in us long before anyone (including us) can see it. The work is happening even when the landscape looks unchanged. And when the season shifts, what God planted begins to show.

I'm reminded of a story:  A woman bought a brand‑new car loaded with features; heated seats, lane assist, remote start, voice commands. Months later, her friend got in and said, ‘Why are you scraping ice off your windshield? Just use the remote start.’

She blinked and said, ‘It has that?’

He pressed a button, and the car lit up like a Christmas tree.

She said, ‘I’ve been freezing for no reason!

Many of you will hear that and think the point of the story is the woman, her lack of knowledge of how to use her car's features.  Some of you will think it's about the friend knowing her car better than she does.  However today, the point is the car.  Are you like the car?  Are you full of God-given features, gifts and purpose, yet not living a full life because you haven't activated what God already installed?

Here is where we meet Jonah in the Bible.  Jonah was a fully loaded prophet.  God had already given him purpose, "Go to Nineveh".  God had already given him the gift, a prophetic voice.  But, none of that potential activated until Jonah gave God obedience.  He wasn't missing calling.  He wasn't missing anointing.  He wasn't missing ability.  What he was missing was alignment.  God wasn't using his features and God had to get him in order.

Jonah 1:1-3
Jonah Flees the Presence of the LORD  
​1 Now the word of the LORD came to Jonah the son of Amittai, saying, 2 “Arise, go to Nineveh, that great city, and call out against it, for their evil has come up before me.” 3 But Jonah rose to flee to Tarshish from the presence of the LORD. He went down to Joppa and found a ship going to Tarshish. So he paid the fare and went down into it, to go with them to Tarshish, away from the presence of the LORD.

He was really trying to get away wasn't he.  The last three sentences there are repetitive to express and emphasize just how much Jonah was really trying to get away.  I don't know that I blame him.  This was a big job and not necessarily a pretty one.  Nineveh, the capital of Assyria, is called the "Great City" for a reason.  This place is huge.  It's a large bustling metropolitan area.  It was on the east bank of the Tigris, bound on the other side by mountains.  You can think of it like Seattle and the Greater Seattle area.  If I understand correctly it extends about 25mi down the Tigris and 15mi across, with other cities making up this greater area.  It was rumored to be greater than Babylon and was the center of power, governance, and influence in the Gentile world.  It had a reputation for decapitating people, displaying heads on walls, and skinning people alive.  It was not a nice place. It had become wicked, so wicked that a report was given to God.  So, Jonah wasn't running from some small assignment.  He didn't see how he could have an impact.  What Jonah saw was a big wicked and unchangeable beast.  Thankfully God sees potential where we see problems.

What's interesting is that this is not a story of discovering potential.  Jonah had used his gift to great affect before.  He appears in 2 Kings.

2 Kings 14:25
Jeroboam II Reigns in Israel: Jonah had used his gift before
25 He restored the border of Israel from Lebo-hamath as far as the Sea of the Arabah, according to the word of the LORD, the God of Israel, which he spoke by his servant Jonah the son of Amittai, the prophet, who was from Gath-hepher. 

So by the time Jonah is commanded to go to Nineveh, he's not new to this.  Jonah has been that guy.  He's known as that guy.  He knows exactly the gifts he has, but when it comes to an assignment he doesn't like, Jonah makes a choice.  He all of a sudden doesn't think he can do the job God has given him.  Where God see's potential, he see's a problem.  Jonah forgets the gift was never about him and what he wanted.  Jonah put himself between God's call and the assignment.  Some of you may have the spirit of Jonah today.  You've forgotten that God gave you that voice; those hands; that knowledge; that car; whatever it is.  Understand that your potential is not about what you have already done.  It's about what God wants to do through you.

We must remember that God gives gifts, capacities, and assignments that align with His purpose.

Romans 12:6-8
Gifts of Grace
6 Having gifts that differ **according to the grace given to us, let us use them**: if prophecy, in proportion to our faith; 7 if service, in our serving; the one who teaches, in his teaching; 8 the one who exhorts, in his exhortation; the one who contributes, in generosity; the one who leads, with zeal; the one who does acts of mercy, with cheerfulness.


So we see here that those gifts can be in your speaking, in your doing, in what you have learned, in your generosity, and in your cheer.  Being cheerful in this world is a gift and you owe it to the saints.

1 Corinthians 12:7
Spiritual Gifts
7 To each is given the manifestation of the Spirit **for the common good**.


What God put in you, was never about you.
